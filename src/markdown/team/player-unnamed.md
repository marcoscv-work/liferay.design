### How did you get into design?

I started modding game UIs when I was 14. I'd spend hours tweaking health bars, inventory screens, and minimap layouts. One day I realized I was spending more time designing interfaces than actually playing the games.

### Why are you at Liferay?

I wanted to work on something that matters at scale. Enterprise software touches millions of people every day, and most of it looks like it was designed in 2005. I'm here to change that.

### Favorite tool?

Figma, but I keep a sketchbook next to my keyboard for when I need to think without pixels.

### Best lifehack?

The two-minute rule: if something takes less than two minutes, do it now. It keeps my task list from becoming a graveyard.

### Where do you find #inspiration?

Video game UI design, retro computing aesthetics, and watching how people interact with kiosks and ATMs in public.

### One thing you are getting better at?

Saying "no" to feature creep. Every pixel has to earn its place on the screen.

### If you weren't designing, what would you be doing?

Game development. I'd probably be an indie dev making weird little puzzle games with hand-drawn art.

### Favorite place to visit?

Tokyo. The signage, the vending machines, the train station wayfinding — it's a masterclass in information design.

### Favorite hotkey?

Ctrl + Z. Not just in design — I wish life had undo.

### Advice for ambitious designers?

Ship things. A finished project that's 80% perfect teaches you more than a concept that's 100% perfect but never sees the light of day.

### Last book you read?

"The Design of Everyday Things" by Don Norman. I re-read it every year and find something new each time.

### Most effective productivity booster?

Noise-canceling headphones and a lo-fi playlist. Instant focus mode.

### What keeps you up at night?

Wondering if the dropdown menu I shipped today will confuse someone tomorrow.
